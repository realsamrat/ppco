We offer a range of photography packages at Portland Picture Company because different sessions genuinely require different things. A 30-minute headshot session and a full-day wedding are not the same service — and neither are the people who need them.

Here's how to think through which option is right for you.

## Start With How You'll Use the Images

This is the most useful question to start with. Not "how many photos do I want" but "where are these photos going to live?"

If you need a single great LinkedIn headshot, a focused 30-minute session delivers everything you need. If you're launching a new brand and need images for a website, social media, a press kit, and a pitch deck — you need a half-day branding package with multiple environments and outfit changes.

Think about the use cases first, then work backwards to the package.

## Duration vs. Coverage

More time doesn't always mean more images — it means more situations, more variety, and more opportunity for the session to breathe. An engagement session that has ninety minutes to work with will produce noticeably richer results than one squeezed into forty-five minutes, even if the final edited count is similar.

For weddings, coverage duration is the critical variable. Do you need getting-ready footage? A first look? Full reception coverage? Each adds time, and we'd rather discuss this honestly upfront than have you realize on the day that you booked too little.

## What's Included: The Questions to Ask

- **Edited images delivered**: How many? In what resolution?
- **Turnaround time**: When do you get your gallery?
- **Print rights**: Can you print freely or are there restrictions?
- **Second photographer**: For weddings especially, does the package include one?
- **Online gallery**: How long is it hosted?

All Portland Picture Company packages include full digital rights, a delivered online gallery, and no restrictions on printing.

## When Budget Is a Real Constraint

Be honest with us about your budget. We'd rather build a session that fits your actual situation than have you stretch for a package that creates stress. We can almost always find an approach that works.
