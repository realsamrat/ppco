The Thompsons didn't want a photoshoot. They wanted an evening at the mountain with their kids — and they wanted someone there to capture it. That's the brief we work best with.

We met at Trillium Lake ninety minutes before sunset. Mt. Hood was out, the lake was still, and the kids were already running toward the water before we'd even said hello.

## Let Them Run

Our philosophy at Portland Picture Company for family sessions: get out of the way first. Let the kids be kids. Let the parents relax. The best images come from the first fifteen minutes when everyone forgets we're there.

The Thompson boys spent a solid twenty minutes skipping rocks and trying to splash each other. We were fifty feet away with a long lens, just watching. Those frames — the genuinely caught moments of joy — are the ones their parents will print large.

## Pulling the Family Together

Once everyone had burned off some energy, we started working. Nothing complicated — walk toward us, sit on that log, mom and dad pick up the little one. The goal is always to create situations that generate real reactions, not manufactured smiles.

At one point the youngest found a frog. That bought us another ten minutes of completely authentic material.

## The Golden Hour Meadow

As the sun dropped behind the peak, we moved to a meadow nearby where the grasses glow in that last warm light. This is the window — maybe twenty minutes — where every frame looks like it was shot on film. Warm tones, long shadows, everything soft.

The Thompsons left with muddy boots and tired kids. Their gallery had over four hundred selects from a ninety-minute session.
