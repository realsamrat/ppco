Brand photography is one of the highest-leverage investments a small business can make. The right images will work for you on your website, your social media, your pitch decks, and your press coverage for the next two to three years.

But the session itself is only half the work. Here's how to prepare so you get maximum value out of your time with Portland Picture Company.

## Define Your Visual Identity First

Before the session, get clear on what your brand feels like. Pull together a small mood board — five to ten images that represent the aesthetic you're going for. Warm and approachable? Cool and editorial? Earthy and organic?

This doesn't need to be a formal document. A saved Instagram collection works fine. But having it gives us a shared language to work from on the day.

## Build a Shot List

Think about every place you'll use these images:

- **Website**: Hero image, about page, services page
- **Social media**: Grid posts, stories, profile photo
- **LinkedIn**: Professional headshot, cover photo
- **Press kit**: Speaker bio photo, media mentions

Work backwards from that list to decide what types of shots you need — headshots, workspace shots, lifestyle images, product details, behind-the-scenes.

We'll refine this together in our pre-session call, but coming in with a rough list means we don't waste time on the day.

## Wardrobe: Bring Options, Not Just One Look

Three to five outfits gives you variety across different use cases. Think about your audience — what does your ideal client expect you to look like? Lead with that, then add one or two looks that show personality.

Solid colors and simple textures photograph cleanest. Avoid loud patterns or anything with visible logos unless that's intentional.

## Prep Your Workspace

If we're shooting in your office, studio, or home office, take an hour the day before to style the space. Clear the clutter, add a few intentional objects — books, plants, things that are relevant to your work. The background tells part of your story.

## Relax on the Day

Your best branding images aren't going to come from holding a perfect pose. They're going to come from a moment when you're genuinely engaged in something — talking through an idea, working at your desk, laughing with a colleague.

We create those conditions intentionally. Your job is to show up, trust the process, and be yourself.
