The most common thing we hear before a headshot session at Portland Picture Company: *"I'm not very photogenic."*

It's almost never true. What people mean is that they've had bad headshots before — stiff, awkward, lit like a passport photo. The problem wasn't them. It was the process.

Here's how to set yourself up for headshots that actually look like you.

## 1. Wear What You Actually Wear to Work

Not your fanciest outfit. Not something you borrowed. Wear what you'd wear on a day when you have an important meeting and you feel confident.

Solid colors photograph cleanest — avoid busy patterns and logos. Jewel tones (navy, emerald, burgundy) tend to look great on most people. Bring a second option if you're unsure.

## 2. Stop Practicing Your Smile in the Mirror

The mirror smile is the enemy of natural headshots. Instead, think of something that actually makes you laugh — a friend, a memory, a genuinely funny moment. That's what a real smile looks like, and the camera knows the difference immediately.

## 3. Arrive Early and Move Around First

Give yourself ten minutes before we start. Walk around, get comfortable in the space, chat with us. The first frames of any session are always the most stiff — and if we spend a few minutes loosening up before we pick up the camera, those stiff frames never happen.

## 4. Let Us Direct the Light

This is the part that has nothing to do with you — the quality of light completely determines whether a headshot looks editorial or like a DMV photo. At Portland Picture Company we shoot with large, soft natural light sources whenever possible. Your job is to trust that and relax.

## 5. Plan for 30 Minutes, Not 10

Headshot sessions that rush rarely produce the best results. When you have time to settle in, try a few different expressions, and review a few frames together, the images get noticeably better in the second half of the session.
