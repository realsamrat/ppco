Portland is one of the best cities in the country for engagement photography. You've got lush forests five minutes from downtown, a gorgeous waterfront, historic architecture, and — if you're willing to drive thirty minutes — the Columbia River Gorge.

Here are the locations we keep coming back to at Portland Picture Company.

## Forest Park

Over five thousand acres of old-growth forest inside city limits. The Wildwood Trail gives you towering firs, fern-covered floors, and that quintessential Pacific Northwest atmosphere. Best in spring when everything is impossibly green, and in fall when the light goes golden through the canopy.

Pro tip: shoot on an overcast day. The soft, diffused light is incredible in here.

## Trillium Lake at Mt. Hood

If you're willing to make the forty-five minute drive, Trillium Lake delivers some of the most dramatic backdrops in the Pacific Northwest. Mt. Hood's reflection in the still water, wildflowers in summer, snow-capped peaks year-round.

We do a lot of golden hour sessions here. Plan to arrive two hours before sunset.

## The Columbia River Gorge

Latourell Falls, Crown Point, Rowena Crest — the Gorge is endlessly photogenic. The combination of dramatic basalt cliffs, ribbon waterfalls, and river views makes every frame feel cinematic.

This is where we took Sarah and Michael for their [gorge engagement session](/blog/gorge-engagement-session). One of our all-time favorite galleries.

## The Pearl District

For couples who want something more urban and editorial, the Pearl District's cobblestone streets and brick warehouse facades deliver a completely different vibe. Evening sessions here, after the street lights come on, have a romantic glow that's hard to beat.

## Pittock Mansion Grounds

The grounds of Pittock Mansion offer sweeping views of downtown Portland and Mt. Hood on clear days. The manicured gardens, stone walls, and classic architecture give a timeless, slightly formal feel — perfect for couples who want something elegant.

## Powell's City of Books

Quirky, colorful, and completely Portland. For book lovers, the stacks at Powell's make a backdrop unlike anything else. Limited light to work with, but the right couple in here produces images that are uniquely theirs.
