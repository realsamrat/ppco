Some couples are made for studios. Sarah and Michael are made for the Gorge.

When they booked their engagement session with Portland Picture Company, they didn't want manicured gardens or urban backdrops. They wanted waterfalls, forest trails, and room to move. We drove out to Latourell Falls on a September afternoon and delivered exactly that.

## The Falls

We started at the base of Latourell Falls, where the mist from the 249-foot drop creates a constant soft haze in the air. It's atmospheric in a way you can't manufacture in a studio — the kind of light that makes images feel like paintings.

Sarah wore a flowing ivory dress that moved beautifully in the breeze off the falls. Michael kept her laughing for basically the entire shoot, which is all you can ask for.

## The Hike Up

After the falls, we made the short climb to the upper viewpoint. The Columbia River stretched out below us, the basalt walls of the Gorge framing the horizon. Late afternoon light was starting to go warm and golden — the kind of light we chase.

We worked quickly up here. The best light in the Gorge doesn't last long.

## Golden Hour in the Meadow

On the drive back we pulled over at a meadow we'd scouted earlier in the day. The grasses were backlit, turning the whole field amber. Sarah and Michael walked, danced a little, and mostly just existed in the moment together. Some of our cleanest, most quietly beautiful frames from the whole session came from this ten-minute stop.
