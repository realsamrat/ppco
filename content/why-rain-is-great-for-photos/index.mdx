Every week between October and April, someone texts us asking whether they should reschedule because of rain.

The answer is almost always no.

## The Pacific Northwest Secret Every Photographer Knows

Overcast skies are a giant diffusion panel for the sun. Instead of harsh midday light with deep shadows under eyes and blown-out highlights, you get soft, even illumination that wraps around faces beautifully. No squinting. No harsh shadows. No chasing patches of shade.

It's the light every portrait photographer is trying to recreate in a studio — and the Pacific Northwest delivers it for free, for six months a year.

## Wet Pavement Is a Location Upgrade

Rain transforms Portland's streets. Reflective puddles mirror the sky and ambient light. Brick sidewalks go deep and rich. The colors in Forest Park saturate to something almost unrealistic.

We have sessions from rainy November days that we'd put up against anything shot in peak summer conditions.

## The Mood Is Different — In a Good Way

There's a quality to rainy-day portraits that's hard to manufacture otherwise. Couples huddled under umbrellas. A family running laughing between trees. The intimacy of two people sharing a coat.

If you're going for moody, romantic, or cinematic — overcast is your friend.

## What to Prepare For

We're not saying show up unprepared. A few things that help:

- **Bring an umbrella** — not just for shelter, but as a prop. Brightly colored umbrellas especially.
- **Wear layers** so you can regulate temperature
- **Rubber boots** if you have them — wet socks kill the vibe
- **Trust us on timing** — we know when the light is at its best even on grey days

## When We Actually Do Recommend Rescheduling

Thunderstorms. Sustained heavy downpour with no break in the forecast. Those are the conditions where we'll proactively reach out about moving the date. Light rain and overcast? We're going.
