Senior portraits have changed completely in the last five years. The stiff studio backdrop, the awkward cap-and-gown setup, the formulaic poses — largely gone. What's replaced them is genuinely exciting, and it's made our senior sessions at Portland Picture Company some of the most creative work we do.

Here's what the Class of 2024 is bringing to their sessions.

## Authenticity Over Polish

Today's seniors don't want to look like a stock photo. They want images that look like *them* — their style, their energy, their actual personality. That means if you wear streetwear every day, you're not showing up in a blazer for portraits. If you're a musician, you're bringing your guitar.

We lean into this completely. The brief for every senior session is: show us who you actually are.

## Multiple Locations, One Session

Rather than stationary setups, seniors are choosing sessions that move through two or three locations. Forest → urban alley → rooftop. The variety of backgrounds and lighting gives the gallery a range that works across different yearbook formats, social media, and large prints.

## Props That Tell a Story

Skateboards, cameras, lacrosse sticks, art supplies, a beloved dog — anything that connects to your life belongs in your senior portraits. The images that hold up over decades are the specific ones, not the generic ones.

## Film-Inspired Editing

Warm tones, slight grain, organic color grading — the film aesthetic is everywhere in senior portrait photography right now. It ages well and it feels genuinely artistic rather than over-processed.

## Natural Environments

Portland is perfect for this. Forest Park, the Gorge, Mt. Tabor, the waterfront — every senior in this city has access to incredible natural light environments within twenty minutes of home.
